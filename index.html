I'm a Biophysics PhD student at UC Berkeley in Jill Banfield's lab. My research interests broadly lie in understanding the sequence-to-structure evolutionary landscape of proteins, particularly those found in exciting microbes from diverse environments.

I completed my B.Sc. at UC Berkeley in Chemical Biology, with a double minor in Data Science and Bioengineering. As an undergraduate, I developed functional annotation methods for uncharacterized fungal and algal proteins at the Joint Genome Institute. I also pursued chemical biology research in the Francis Lab and at Aralez Bio. Prior to starting graduate school, I spent a wonderful summer working at the Artificial Intelligence Research Institute in Tokyo, Japan.

I grew up in Hong Kong and now reside in the beautiful San Francisco Bay Area. My hobbies include making ceramics, hiking and playing board games.

Feel free to contact me via email (kinsey at berkeley dot edu), LinkedIn or Bluesky!
